A Legacy of Global Leadership

Chevening is the UK government’s international awards programme aimed at developing global leaders. Funded by the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office and partner organisations, it offers a unique opportunity for future leaders, influencers, and decision-makers from all over the world to develop professionally and academically.

By studying in the UK, you will be part of a vibrant, diverse community and experience the highest standards of academic excellence.

Eligibility Criteria

Do You Have What It Takes?

Chevening is looking for ambitious individuals with a clear vision for the future. Review our core requirements below.

✓

Citizenship: Must be a citizen of a Chevening-eligible country or territory.

✓

Commitment: Must return to your home country for at least two years after study.

✓

Academic: Undergraduate degree equivalent to at least a UK 2:1 honours degree.

✓

Experience: Have at least two years (2,800 hours) of professional work experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply if I already have a scholarship? +
Chevening awards cannot be held in conjunction with any other scholarship funded by the UK government. If you are offered another scholarship, you must choose between them.
Is work experience mandatory? +
Yes, a minimum of two years (2,800 hours) is mandatory. This includes full-time, part-time, voluntary work, and paid or unpaid internships.
Can I choose any UK university? +
You can apply to any valid taught Master's program at any university in the UK. However, the award covers up to a certain threshold for MBA courses.
What subjects can I study? +
There is no restriction on subjects. You are encouraged to choose courses that align with your leadership goals and your country's development needs.
When do applications open? +
Applications typically open in August each year and close in November for the following academic year. Early preparation is key.
How competitive is Chevening? +
It is highly competitive. Every year, more than 50,000 applications are received for roughly 1,500 to 1,800 scholarship spots.
Can I bring my family? +
Chevening does not provide financial support for dependents. Scholars who bring family must prove they have the funds to support them.
What happens after the scholarship? +
Scholars are expected to return home for at least two years to apply their new skills and contribute to their home country's development.
